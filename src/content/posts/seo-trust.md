Most small business owners think SEO is about rankings. It's not. It's about whether a stranger trusts you enough to call. Rankings are just the mechanism that gets them to the page. What happens after that is entirely about trust signals — reviews, photos, a website that doesn't look abandoned, copy that sounds like a human wrote it. The businesses that win local search aren't always the ones with the best optimization. They're the ones that look the most trustworthy at a glance.
